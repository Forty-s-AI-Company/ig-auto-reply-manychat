# Product

## Register

product

## Users

InboxPilot serves creators, small commerce teams, social agencies, and lean operators who manage Instagram comments, DMs, customer tags, and follow-up workflows. They usually work under campaign pressure, switching between customer support, sales, and content operations, and they need a workspace that makes the next action obvious without requiring technical setup knowledge.

## Product Purpose

InboxPilot turns Instagram comments and messages into trackable, assignable, automatable conversations. Success means a user can connect an Instagram account, understand inbox status, create the first useful automation, and see whether messages, contacts, and workflows are healthy without needing to learn a broad marketing automation platform first.

## Brand Personality

Clear, practical, and quietly confident. The product should feel like an operations partner for Chinese-speaking teams: approachable enough for non-technical users, precise enough for SaaS operators, and restrained enough to earn trust around customer messages and platform integrations.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI SaaS landing pages with decorative gradients, repeated identical feature cards, vague "all-in-one growth" claims, and over-polished glass effects. Avoid dashboard UI that feels like a theme demo: arbitrary shadows, mismatched icon styles, raw hex colors scattered across components, and status colors that rely on color alone.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the user's next operational step, not the product's feature inventory.
2. Keep Instagram-first flows obvious in the simple release; broader platform capability belongs in staging/full release.
3. Use familiar SaaS patterns for dashboard work so users can focus on messages, contacts, and automations.
4. Make system state and risk visible: connection health, DB health, unfinished setup, and missing env values should never be hidden.
5. Use warm product copy, but keep UI controls concise and action-oriented.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for text contrast, keyboard navigation, focus visibility, and form labeling. Support reduced motion for landing animations and dashboard transitions. Do not communicate health, success, warning, or error states by color alone; pair color with labels, icons, or explicit text. Traditional Chinese should be treated as the primary content language, with English as a secondary option where needed.
